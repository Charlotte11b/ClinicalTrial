# Example Safety Escalation Guidance - Replace Before Training Use

Escalate immediately when a participant reports:

- Severe or worsening symptoms.
- Chest pain, trouble breathing, fainting, new neurologic symptoms, severe allergic reaction, suicidal thoughts, or other urgent symptoms.
- Hospitalization, emergency visit, or life-threatening event.
- Pregnancy or other protocol-defined urgent safety issue when relevant.

Do not provide independent medical advice. Use the approved script to direct the participant to urgent care, emergency services, the study clinician, or PI.
